HISTORY

Establishment of Tokugawa Shogunate

Tokugawa Ieyasu establishes the Tokugawa Shogunate in Edo (Tokyo's original name), leading to its growth as a major political and cultural center in Japan.

Tokugawa Ieyasu
Image Source: Britannica

Meiji Restoration

The Meiji Restoration brings an end to the Tokugawa Shogunate, and the Emperor moves to Edo, which is renamed Tokyo, becoming the new capital of Japan.

Emperor Meiji
Image Source: Britannica

Assimilation of Western Civilization

Japan initiates efforts to adopt Western civilization, including infrastructure development, modern communication, and Western fashion trends.

Infrastructure was developed during the Meiji Restoration, including steam locomotives
Image Source: Government of Japan

Great Kanto Earthquake

The Great Kanto Earthquake strikes Tokyo, resulting in extensive destruction, over 140,000 casualties, and 300,000 destroyed houses.

Destruction from the Great Kanto Earthquake
Image Source: Britannica

Tokyo during World War II

The Pacific War and numerous bombings significantly impact Tokyo, leading to the merger of Tokyo-fu and Tokyo-shi, forming the Metropolis of Tokyo.

Firebombing of Tokyo occurred during WWII
Image Source: CNN

Tokyo's New Governance System

Japan adopts a new constitution and local autonomy law, leading to the establishment of Tokyo's special ward system.

Image of the new constitution of Japan
Image Source: Library of Congress

Tokyo Olympics and Modernization

Tokyo hosts the Olympic Games, and significant infrastructure developments, including the Shinkansen and the Metropolitan Expressway, contribute to Tokyo's prosperity.

Image of the Tokyo Olympics 1964
Image Source: Olympics

Economic Slowdown and Environmental Concerns

The Oil Crisis halts rapid economic growth, and Tokyo faces environmental challenges like pollution and noise.

The oil crisis was a global phenomenon, affecting all countries including Japan
Image Source: Malevus

Tokyo's Economic Boom and "Bubble Economy"

Tokyo experiences economic growth, emerging as a global city with advanced technology, information, culture, and fashion.

Japan had a major economic boom in the 1980s, which is indicated by this graph of land prices
Image Source: Voss Capital

Burst of the Bubble Economy

The 1990s mark the end of the "bubble economy," leading to a financial crisis and protracted economic slump.

The bubble economy burst in 1990, leading to a sharp recession in Japan and Tokyo, as indicated by the stock prices
Image Source: GRIP

Tokyo Affected by the Great East Japan Earthquake

The Great East Japan Earthquake strikes the Tohoku region and also impacts Tokyo, prompting efforts to strengthen the city's crisis management system.

Destruction from the Great East Japan Earthquake
Image Source: The Atlantic

Tokyo Chosen as Olympic Host City

Tokyo is selected to host its second Olympic and Paralympic Games in 2020, leading to initiatives for infrastructure upgrades and environmental measures.

Image of Tokyo 2020 Olympics
Image Source: Malevus

Projected Peak Population

Tokyo's population is projected to reach its peak in 2020, after which it is expected to start declining due to changes in demographics.

Graph showing population of Tokyo over time
Image Source: Tokyo Metropolitan Government
